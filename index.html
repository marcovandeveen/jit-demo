Key Metrics
Render Rate
Ads impressed ÷ Ads served

Measures delivery efficiency. A served ad only generates revenue when it is actually seen. An ad stitched into a manifest that no viewer reaches consumes resources without returning any revenue.

JIT improves render rate by stitching ads at playback time, only for viewers who reach that break, rather than pre-stitching ads into every manifest regardless of whether the viewer gets there.

Fill Rate
Ads served ÷ Ads requested

Measures demand strength. A low fill rate means ad requests went unanswered, leaving break time unused with no revenue.

Fill rate is also affected by render rate. When buyers win auctions but receive no impressions, demand-side platforms flag the supply as low-quality, reduce bids, and shift spend elsewhere. Poor render rate compounds into lower fill and revenue.
